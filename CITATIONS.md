## TOPSIS (Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution)

### Original Publication

**Authors:** Ching-Lai Hwang and Kwangsun Yoon  
**Year:** 1981

**Full Citation:**
```
Hwang, C. L., & Yoon, K. (1981). Multiple Attribute Decision Making: Methods and Applications. 
Lecture Notes in Economics and Mathematical Systems, Vol. 186. Springer-Verlag, New York.
ISBN: 978-3-540-10558-9
```

**About the Authors:**
- **Ching-Lai Hwang:** Professor at Kansas State University, pioneer in MCDM
- **Kwangsun Yoon:** Contributed significantly to multi-attribute decision theory

**Impact:** One of the most widely used MCDM methods, cited in thousands of research papers across engineering, business, and social sciences.

## MEREC (Method based on Removal Effects of Criteria)

### Original Publication

**Authors:** Mehdi Keshavarz-Ghorabaee, Mohammad Amiri, Edmundas Kazimieras Zavadskas, Zenonas Turskis  
**Year:** 2021

**Full Citation:**
```
Keshavarz-Ghorabaee, M., Amiri, M., Zavadskas, E. K., & Turskis, Z. (2021). 
Determination of Objective Weights Using a New Method Based on the Removal Effects of Criteria (MEREC). 
Symmetry, 13(4), 525.
DOI: 10.3390/sym13040525
```

**About the Authors:**
- **Mehdi Keshavarz-Ghorabaee:** Researcher in decision-making methods
- **Edmundas Kazimieras Zavadskas:** Prominent Lithuanian researcher, highly cited in MCDM field
- **Zenonas Turskis:** Expert in construction engineering and management

**Impact:** Recent method (2021) gaining rapid adoption for objective weight determination.

## VIKOR (VIšeKriterijumska Optimizacija I Kompromisno Rešenje)

### Original Publications

**Author:** Serafim Opricović  
**Years:** 1979 (Ph.D. dissertation), 1998 (book), 2004 (international paper)

**Full Citations:**
```
Opricovic, S. (1998). Multicriteria Optimization of Civil Engineering Systems. 
Faculty of Civil Engineering, Belgrade, Serbia. (in Serbian)

Opricovic, S., & Tzeng, G. H. (2004). Compromise solution by MCDM methods: 
A comparative analysis of VIKOR and TOPSIS. 
European Journal of Operational Research, 156(2), 445-455.
DOI: 10.1016/S0377-2217(03)00020-1
```

**About the Authors:**
- **Serafim Opricović:** Serbian researcher who developed VIKOR in his 1979 Ph.D. dissertation
- **Gwo-Hshiung Tzeng:** Taiwanese scholar, highly influential in MCDM research

**Impact:** The 2004 paper became one of the most cited papers in economics. VIKOR is particularly popular in engineering and environmental decision-making.

```

DOI: 10.3390/su8040372
```

**About the Authors:**
- **Dragan Pamučar:** Serbian researcher specializing in decision-making and logistics
- **Ljubomir Gigović:** Expert in GIS and spatial decision-making

**Year:** 1995

**Full Citation:**
```
Diakoulaki, D., Mavrotas, G., & Papayannakis, L. (1995). 
Determining objective weights in multiple criteria problems: The CRITIC method. 
Computers & Operations Research, 22(7), 763-770.
DOI: 10.1016/0305-0548(94)00059-H
```

**About the Authors:**
- **Danae Diakoulaki:** Greek researcher in energy systems and decision analysis
- **George Mavrotas:** Expert in multi-objective optimization
- **Lefteris Papayannakis:** Economist and policy analyst

**Impact:** Widely used for objective weight determination, especially when criteria correlation matters.

## Entropy Method

### Foundational Work

**Author:** Claude Elwood Shannon  
**Year:** 1948

**Full Citation:**
```
Shannon, C. E. (1948). A mathematical theory of communication. 
Bell System Technical Journal, 27(3), 379-423.
DOI: 10.1002/j.1538-7305.1948.tb01338.x
```

**About the Author:**
- **Claude Shannon:** American mathematician, "father of information theory"
- Revolutionized communications and laid foundation for digital age

### MCDM Application

**Author:** Milan Zeleny  
**Year:** 1982

**Full Citation:**
```
Zeleny, M. (1982). Multiple Criteria Decision Making. 
McGraw-Hill, New York.
ISBN: 978-0070727953
```

**About the Author:**
- **Milan Zeleny:** Czech-American professor, pioneer in MCDM
- Adapted Shannon's entropy concept for decision-making weight determination

**Impact:** Entropy-based weighting is one of the most fundamental objective weighting methods in MCDM.
